Time required: Preparation 2 min; implementation about 20 minutes along with employees

If you as a manager understand your employees' different preferences and requirements increases your ability to find ways to celebrate success in a way that motivates employees.

One can celebrate in many ways - and what is perceived as relevant vary between individuals. This exercise is to find out more about how your employees appreciate being celebrated. You can do this in several ways.

  1. Consider yourself celebrations over which you have tried and which of them has had the best effect.
  2. Listen to what your employees say that they like to do.
  3. Led a conversation about what you could do to celebrate such you done good. Quit eg a meeting by asking employees to suggest how they would like you to celebrate successes. You can either do it individually or in groups during a particleboard
    1. Write down "How can we do to celebrate something we done well?" So that all participants see it.
    2. Generate as many different suggestions on ways to celebrate as possible. Participants can either write individually on post-it notes (one proposal per patch) or on a blank piece of paper, or together on paper or white board or the like.
    3. Please rank the proposals.
