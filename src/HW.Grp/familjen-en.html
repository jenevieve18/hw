Time: 40-60 minutes.

Audience: HR process leader for executive groups (10-20 managers).

The following exercise is carried out in connection with cutbacks, tentatively led by HR. The aim is to raise awareness among managers about the choices, feelings and ethical issues they may be facing. Through increased understanding of the consequences (eg, reactions and emotions) of the different roles so managers can better prepare for cutbacks. As facilitator leads you exercise and the subsequent discussions and helps participants to discuss experiences.

  1. Start by dividing the heads of the three groups.
  2. Tell managers that they are now a family and the parents in the family gathers his children to tell them that the family has financial problems. They now no longer afford to buy food for everyone in the family and that they therefore need to give away some of the children.
  3. It is now time to hand out roles. One group consists of parents, the second of the children who remain in the family, and the third consists of those who are being left out.
  4. Each group gets over about 10 minutes to sit and discuss how it feels to be part of the group they are in.
  5. Then each group tell the others how it feels to be in that group.
  6. After each group has reported, you can have a joint discussion about how best to support each group. Switch from a family perspective to a workgroup or organisationsperspektiv.Nedan are examples of what you might think of when discussing.

NOTE! This exercise can arouse strong reactions of some participants. It is therefore important to be vigilant and take care of these participants' reactions. There is a drawback to exercise that needs to be discussed with the participants after the exercise has been completed. Family The example means that it can be difficult to get the perspective that some people might perceive it as something positive to leave a job. In reality, there may be individuals who will be relieved and happy that they are able to for a fresh start in life and devote himself to other things they would rather wish. The reaction is difficult to translate into family example.

If a group is difficult to reflect and problematize, eg only conclude that it feels good to be left in the organization, you as facilitator to enable people in the other of the groups. You can also ask a few follow-up questions to stimulate more perspective.
