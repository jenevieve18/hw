Time: 5-10 minutes

Every success that your group provides an opportunity for you to focus on results and efforts in a positive way. Below are some questions that you can reflect on when you plan your celebration. Followed by guidelines which ensure that the celebration is truly motivating for employees.

Reflection Questions:

Reflect for a moment on how you see the questions below. You can then take part in more information about each issue.

Why should you celebrate success?

It gives your employees the opportunity to pause and reflect on where they are and what has taken them there. It will be a natural opportunity not only to enjoy but also to analyze success factors and spontaneously reinforce what worked well.

What is appropriate to celebrate?

Generally you can say that you can celebrate the results or efforts. Most often result easiest to see and pay attention. Sometimes you may also like to celebrate the efforts that they believe will lead to results in the long term. This is particularly appropriate when it goes a long time before the results come. It may for example be about one rung more customers than before, started to work according to a new process, handled a difficult period, and so on.

How will the celebration take place?

When you know what is worth celebrating is to find the right way to celebrate. The challenge is to find ways to celebrate that are perceived as relevant for your employees. Do not be fooled that "the bigger the better". Rather, there is a risk of too grandiose celebrations: namely, habituation factor. We have celebrated in a grand way once, it may become a baseline against which future celebration is compared (and then fall short). Small celebrations will also be easier to do many times, which is good for the motivating effect. For the celebration, apply the rule: "Sm�slaget, personal and so often there is a reason."

How personal can you get?

Be personal in your thanks
What you do is not important, but that's how it is presented. A fervently expressed thanks matters more than the content of most gifts. No matter what style of leadership you have, this is a great time to be personal - let your employees know what their performance has meant to you. Describe genuine and honest appreciation for the work done - and was careful to describe the behavior, what they have done. If you want to mention people by name - make sure you say a sentence or two about each one's contribution. In this way you show that you have been attentive to each one's contribution. Help your employees to see how they developed, both as individuals and teams. Remind them where they were from the beginning, and where they are now.

Should others outside the group concerned?

Let others know
Nothing clarifies praise and appreciation more than amplification. Presenting the group's achievement at a rally. Write about it in the staff magazine. Send the invitation to the celebration, including a detailed description of the feat, and cc: A senior managers. Invite them too!

How can the celebration itself be developing?

Listen carefully
when celebrations underway: listen carefully! This casual situation can invite more forthright conversations than during formal project meetings. Listen and learn from the experience you will receive.

Use the opportunity to give feedback to each other
to start is to talk myself but then let the employees themselves take on to describe how they have done to succeed in what you are celebrating. Set some initial questions like how did you do to succeed? What problems did you meet along the way and how did you solve them?
